Valerie Odonkor

FRENCH EDITOR

Hi! My name is Valerie Odonkor, and I am a junior double majoring in Government and French and minoring in international relations and public policy within the College of Arts and Sciences. In my free time, I like keeping up with world news, reading, creative writing, discovering new music, and taking pictures everywhere I go. I’ve been able to hone this passion for photography through photojournalism, shooting for the Cornell Daily Sun and artistically shooting for Cornell Thread fashion magazine. I also love volunteering! Currently, I volunteer through the Mario Einaudi Center for International Studies to teach kids in the local area the beautiful and historically rich language of French, the country of France and the diverse Francophone world. I’ve worked as a human rights intern, and am very interested in pursuing human rights in my future law school studies. I hope to pursue this passion specifically within the Francophone world, as speaking French and being involved with French and Francophone culture has become somewhat of a passion of mine. The beauty of this magazine for me lies in its fresh approach to learning about different cultures and perspectives within the art of writing and within respective languages! I, personally can’t wait to get started!
